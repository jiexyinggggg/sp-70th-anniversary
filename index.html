Singapore Polytechnic (SP) is a pre-eminent tertiary education institution that has produced distinguished graduates since its establishment in October 1954.
For 70 years, SP has been at the forefront of education, fostering innovation, creativity, and a spirit of excellence.
SP's alumni have made significant contributions to various industries, both locally and globally.
Since its inception, SP has committed to providing a holistic education that nurtures academic excellence, personal growth, and social responsibility.
SP's diverse range of courses and programs equip students with the know-how and skills they need to thrive in their chosen fields.
SP's hands-on learning and industry-relevant training ensure that our graduates are well-prepared for the ever-changing, modern workforce today.
As SP celebrates their 70th anniversary, we look back with pride at SP's rich history, the achievements of SP's alumni and SP's aspirations.

Industry Disruptions

The whole universe of industry has become much more disruptive, with game-changing developments in areas such as artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning, the Internet of Things (IoT), and robotics. To be future-ready, SP will need to embrace, and adapt to, change in industry – whether from technological advances or other shifts in the workplace. Global Shifts

Of all the global issues facing the world, for most people, climate change tops the list. This subject is huge too, at Singapore Polytechnic, as efforts to help students become more “future-ready” focus on addressing sustainability, always from a holistic, big-picture perspective. This commitment is most concrete in the Polytechnic’s Sustainable Innovation Project (SIP), one of the 10 modules of the Common Core Curriculum. This is a learning experience that culminates in a capstone project that students work on in their second year of study.

SP70 Moments

POLITE Staff Appreciation Day

On 31 October 2023, SP launched its 70th anniversary book titled ‘For All Ages, Singapore Polytechnic At Seventy’ at the Polytechnic-ITE (POLITE) Staff Appreciation Day. The book chronicles the evolution of polytechnic education in Singapore and encapsulates SP's rich history as the nation's first polytechnic, its achievements, aspirations, and dedication to lifelong learning. The occasion, which was graced by Guest-of-Honour Minister for Education Mr Chan Chun Sing, also marked the commencement of SP's 70th anniversary celebrations.

SP Open House 2024

The SP campus was abuzz this year at our 70th Anniversary Open House. Visitors were treated to school tours to visit our cutting-edge facilities and were thrilled by exciting CCA performances at the Plaza. We also had a very special drone swarming display as part of our inaugural SP Industry & Innovation Showcase.

SP70th Anniversary Music Festival

As part of SP’s 70th Anniversary, we held a music festival at SP. Despite the rain and drizzle, the spirit of those who attended was not dampened. Thousands danced to the music, especially when local artists like Evan Low, Rangga Jones, and Marian Carmel took to the stage. The festival’s aim was to cast a glow on the importance of youth mental wellness and to reinforce that music and mental well-being can go hand-in-hand.

SP70 Charity Golf and Gala Dinner

As part of SP's 70th anniversary celebrations in 2024, seven exciting events are lined up throughout the year. The fourth event featured a Charity Golf at Orchid Country Club, followed by the Gala Dinner, showcasing SP's dedication to supporting two initiatives: financially challenged students in SP and helping other communities in need. Held on 12 July 2024, close to 1,000 staff, students, industry partners, alumni, donors, friends, and some President’s Challenge beneficiaries attended both events.

Let's explore how Singapore Polytechnic has evolved from 1954 to the present day!

SP in 1954

Established since 27th October 1954, Singapore Polytechnic is the first polytechnic in Singapore. The completion of its own five-storey building at Prince Edward Road in 1958 saw an enrolment of 2,800 students registered for 58 courses.

SP now

Singapore Polytechnic is now based at Dover, Singapore, since 1979, and is currently the largest Polytechnic in Singapore-a sprawling campus spanning 38 hectares of lush greenery. With over 235,600 graduates to date, SP has consistently shaped and empowered the workforce of tomorrow!

SP in the past

Back in the early days, Singapore Polytechnic students used textbooks and handwritten notes for their studies. Learning was centered around face-to-face lectures, with frequent visits to the library for research and assignments.

SP in recent years

Today, Singapore Polytechnic students utilize computers and digital resources for their coursework. Learning is facilitated through online platforms, enabling interactive, self-paced learning and digital collaboration.
